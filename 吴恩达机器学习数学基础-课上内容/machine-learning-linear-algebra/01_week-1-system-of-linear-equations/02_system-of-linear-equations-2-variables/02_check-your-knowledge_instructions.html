We want your learning experience to be as efficient as possible. If you already know some linear algebra you might be able to skip some of the lectures in this course. Have a look at the questions below. If you already know how to answer them feel free to skip the videos listed in parentheses.

1. Translate the following description into a system of linear equations. (Videos: System of sentences , System of equations as lines )

Bob has a mixture of apples and oranges of the same size, which he split into two bags. The first bag contains 4 apples and 3 oranges and weighs 700g. The second bag contains 2 apples and 6 oranges and weighs 1000g.

2. Translate the following system of linear equations into the matrix form: (Videos: System of Equations (3x3) )

\[\begin{cases} 5x + 3y + 5z= 6 \\ 7x + 2y + 8z = 17 \\ 4x + 3z = 8 \end{cases}\]

3. Compute the determinant of the following matrix: (Video: The determinant (3x3) )

\[\left[ \begin{array}{ccc} 1 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 3 & 5 \\ 4 & 3 & 8 \end{array} \right]\]

4. Does the matrix above have any linear dependent row? (Videos: Linear Dependence and Independence , Linear Dependence and Independence (3x3) )

5. Is the matrix singular or non singular? (Videos: A geometric notion of singularity , Singular vs Nonsingular matrices , Singular vs Nonsingular matrices (3x3) )
